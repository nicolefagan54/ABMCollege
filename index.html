About Denmark

Denmark is a Scandinavian country known for its Viking heritage, modern design, bicycle-friendly cities, and strong sense of community. It often ranks among the happiest countries in the world thanks to its safety, social support, and high quality of life.

The Danish concept of “hygge”—a feeling of coziness and contentment— is reflected in everything from cafés and homes to city streets and seaside towns.

Top Cities to Visit

Copenhagen

The capital city, famous for Nyhavn’s colorful harbor, Tivoli Gardens, royal palaces, and world-class restaurants. Copenhagen is also one of the most bicycle-friendly cities in the world.

Aarhus

A lively university city on the Jutland peninsula, Aarhus offers modern art museums, sandy beaches, and a charming old town with cobblestone streets and half-timbered houses.

Odense

Birthplace of fairy-tale author Hans Christian Andersen, Odense is full of storybook charm, museums, and cozy cafés that celebrate Denmark’s literary history.

Must-See Attractions

Tivoli Gardens

One of the world’s oldest amusement parks, Tivoli Gardens in Copenhagen combines rides, concerts, gardens, and magical lights—especially beautiful in the evening.

The Little Mermaid

Inspired by Hans Christian Andersen’s famous fairy tale, this iconic statue sits by the waterfront and has become a symbol of Copenhagen.

Kronborg Castle

A UNESCO World Heritage site in Helsingør, Kronborg Castle is known worldwide as the setting of Shakespeare’s Hamlet. Its grand halls and seaside views make it a must-visit.

Danish Food to Try

Smørrebrød

Traditional open-faced sandwiches served on rye bread with toppings like pickled herring, roast beef, eggs, and fresh vegetables. Beautiful to look at and delicious to eat.

Danish Pastries

Flaky, buttery pastries known around the world. Try a cinnamon swirl (kanelsnegl) or a custard-filled pastry with icing.

Frikadeller

Danish meatballs, usually served with potatoes, gravy, and red cabbage. A comforting, home-style dish loved by locals.
